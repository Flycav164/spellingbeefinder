How Spelling Bee Scoring Works

The NYT Spelling Bee challenges you to build words from 7 letters, always including the center letter. Four-letter words score 1 point. Five-letter and longer words score 1 point per letter. Pangrams — words using all 7 letters at least once — earn a 7-point bonus on top. Genius requires 70% of the day's total possible points. Queen Bee means you found every valid word.

SpellingBeeFinder finds every valid word in under a second, shows your maximum possible score, calculates the exact Genius threshold, and labels every pangram so you spot it immediately.

Tips to Reach Genius Every Day

TIP 01
Start with the center letter as your anchor. Every valid word must contain it — build outward from there.
TIP 02
Sweep suffixes: -ING, -ED, -ER, -NESS, -TION turn one base word into three or four valid entries.
TIP 03
Hunt the pangram early. Look for 7+ letter words ending in -TION, -MENT, -LING, or -NESS with the center letter inside.
TIP 04
Repeated vowels signal rare words. Three of the same vowel in the hive almost always means unusual entries worth finding.
