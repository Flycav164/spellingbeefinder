What Is a Spelling Bee Solver?

A Spelling Bee solver is a tool that takes the 7 letters from your daily NYT Spelling Bee puzzle and returns every valid word you can make from them — instantly. You don’t have to think of the words yourself. You enter the letters, click solve, and see the complete list in under a second.

This is different from the NYT’s own hints system. NYT hints tell you how many words exist without revealing them. A solver shows you the actual words. It’s a different tool for a different purpose — and the best ones are completely free, with no subscription required.

How a Spelling Bee Solver Works

The mechanics are straightforward. Our solver maintains a dictionary of valid NYT Spelling Bee words — the same vocabulary the puzzle uses, excluding proper nouns, hyphenated words, and obscure terms that the NYT editors reject. When you enter your 7 letters, the solver runs every word in the dictionary through three filters:

  • Length: the word must be at least 4 letters long
  • Center letter: the word must contain the required center letter (the amber hexagon)
  • Letter set: every letter in the word must appear in your 7 available letters, which can be used as many times as needed

Words that pass all three filters appear in your results. Pangrams — words that use all 7 letters at least once — get an amber star and appear at the top regardless of length. Point values are calculated automatically: 1 point for 4-letter words, 1 point per letter for longer words, plus 7 bonus points for every pangram.

The entire process runs in the browser with no server call. There is no waiting, no spinner, no loading bar. You click Find All Words and results appear instantly because the word dictionary and scoring logic are already loaded on the page.

Hints Mode: Solve Without Spoilers

Not everyone wants the full word list. Some players want just enough of a nudge to find a word themselves. That’s what Hints Mode is for. Switch to the Hints tab in the solver and you’ll see a breakdown of how many words start with each letter — but not the words themselves.

For example: “3 words start with C, 2 words start with M, 1 word starts with A.” That’s enough to know you haven’t found all your C words yet. You can also click Reveal next to any letter to see just those words when you’re completely stuck.

This is the same information the NYT’s official Spelling Bee Buddy provides — but ours is free, requires no subscription, and works in the same tab as your solver rather than requiring a trip to nytimes.com and a login.

When to Use the Full Solver vs. Hints

The right time to use each feature depends on where you are in your puzzle:

  • During play, first half: Play without any help. The challenge is the point.
  • During play, stuck: Switch to Hints Mode. See the letter count breakdown. Keep playing.
  • During play, really stuck on one word: Click Reveal on just that starting letter. You see the words, pick one, close the panel, enter it in NYT, move on.
  • After finishing for the day: Click Find All Words. See everything you missed. Read their definitions. This is how you actually improve.
  • Learning patterns: Switch to Practice Mode. Enter any 7-letter combination to explore what words are possible — great for building vocabulary before the next puzzle.

Practice Mode: Build Vocabulary Beyond Today’s Puzzle

Practice Mode is a feature no other Spelling Bee tool offers. Switch to the Practice tab, enter any 7 letters you choose — not just today’s puzzle — and the solver finds every valid word from that combination.

This is powerful for vocabulary building. You can test letter combinations you know are common in the Spelling Bee, explore what words are possible from suffix-heavy sets, or revisit yesterday’s puzzle without the results page. Players who use Practice Mode regularly start recognizing word families faster and need the solver less over time.
