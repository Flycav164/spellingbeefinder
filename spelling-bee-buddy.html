What Is a Spelling Bee Buddy?

A Spelling Bee Buddy is a companion tool that helps you find valid words in the NYT Spelling Bee puzzle. You enter the 7 letters from your daily puzzle — center letter and all 6 outer letters — and the buddy instantly shows you every valid word, sorted by length, with pangrams highlighted and definitions available on tap.

The term “Spelling Bee Buddy” gets used for two different things, which causes a lot of confusion:

  • The NYT official Buddy — a real-time hints tool built into the NYT Games app. It shows how many words you’re missing by starting letter and length, but requires a paid NYT Games subscription ($5/month or $40/year). It does not reveal the actual words.
  • Independent word helpers — free tools like SpellingBeeFinder.com that solve your 7 letters completely. No subscription, no account, no paywall. Just enter your letters and see every valid word in under a second.

Most people searching for “spelling bee buddy” want the second type — a free tool that actually solves the puzzle, not just hints around the edges. That’s what this guide covers.

Hints Mode: Get Help Without Spoiling the Puzzle

The smartest way to use a Spelling Bee Buddy is Hints Mode. Instead of revealing the full word list, Hints Mode shows you how many words start with each letter in the puzzle — just like the NYT official Buddy does, but completely free.

For example, you might see: “3 words start with C, 2 words start with M, 1 word starts with E.” That’s enough information to jog your memory without spoiling the actual words. You still have to find them yourself.

You can also click Reveal next to any letter to see all the words that start with it — letter by letter, on your own terms. This keeps the challenge intact while making sure you never walk away from a puzzle completely stuck.

Pro Tip

Play the puzzle normally first, then switch to Hints Mode when you’re stuck. Only use Find All Words once you’re done playing for the day and want to confirm Queen Bee or learn the words you missed.

How to Use Practice Mode to Get Better at the Puzzle

One feature independent word helpers have that the NYT official Buddy never will is Practice Mode. Switch to the Practice tab and enter any 7-letter combination you want — the solver shows every valid word from those letters, not just today’s puzzle.

This lets you do things no official tool supports:

  • Explore what words are possible from yesterday’s puzzle after the fact
  • Test letter sets that include common suffixes to build vocabulary patterns
  • Practice with letter combinations that have lots of 7-letter words to sharpen your pangram instincts
  • Learn which rare letter combinations unlock obscure but valid Spelling Bee words

Over time, Practice Mode builds the pattern recognition that makes you faster at the daily puzzle without any helper at all. Players who practice consistently start seeing word families — groups of words that share a root or suffix — that they would never have noticed just playing once a day.

Tips to Reach Genius Rank Every Day

Whether you use Hints Mode or the full solver, these strategies will get you to Genius faster:

Start with the center letter as your anchor

Every valid word must contain the center letter. Use it as the starting point for every word you try. Build outward: what 4-letter words can you make with this letter plus three others? What suffixes can you hang off it?

Sweep suffixes systematically

One of the fastest paths to Genius is suffix sweeping. Pick a base word you found, then try every common ending: -ING, -ED, -ER, -LY, -NESS, -TION, -MENT. A single root word can yield 3 to 5 valid entries this way. The solver confirms which ones are in the word list instantly.

Find the pangram before anything else

The pangram is almost always worth 14 or more points — roughly 10 to 20% of the Genius threshold on its own. Hunt it early. Look for words that use 7 or more letters and include the center letter. Common pangram endings: -TION, -MENT, -LING, -NESS, -ICAL, -ATED.

Check Hints Mode at the halfway point

If you’ve found most words but feel stuck, check Hints Mode before revealing everything. Seeing that you’re missing “2 words starting with M” is often enough to unlock them. Preserve the satisfaction of finding the word yourself while getting just enough direction to keep moving.

Use the daily archive for next-day review

After each puzzle resets, visit the daily answers archive to see every word from yesterday’s puzzle. Read every word you missed and its definition. This is the fastest way to expand your vocabulary for future puzzles.

Does Using a Spelling Bee Buddy Count as Cheating?

This comes up constantly and the answer is: it depends entirely on how you use it and what “cheating” means to you. The NYT Spelling Bee has no official enforcement mechanism — there are no accounts that track whether you used outside help, and the game doesn’t penalize you for it.

The more useful question is whether using a helper actually improves your enjoyment and skill:

  • Using Hints Mode to get unstuck = most players consider this fine. It mirrors the NYT official Buddy experience and keeps the core challenge intact.
  • Checking the full word list after you’re done = helps you learn. You find out which words you missed and why. No spoilers because you already played.
  • Looking up words mid-puzzle = some players do this, some don’t. There’s no wrong answer. The puzzle is for your enjoyment, not your exam score.

The players who improve fastest use helpers as a learning tool, not a crutch. They play first, then check. Over time they need the solver less and less because their vocabulary actually grows.
